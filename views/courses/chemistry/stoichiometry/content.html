Stoichiometry

Chemical Formulas

Chemical formulas show the number of each type of atom present in a molecule.

  • H2O: 2 hydrogen atoms are bonded to 1 oxygen atom
  • CO32-: 3 oxygen atoms are bonded to 1 carbon atom producing a compound with a charge of -2
  • Al2O3: an ionic compound with 2 aluminium atoms for every 3 oxygen atoms

Ionic Formulas

The ratio of atoms in an ionic compound depends on the valency of the atoms. Ions with different charges will not have a 1 to 1 ratio.

Al2O3

Aluminium ions have a charge of 3 while oxygen atoms have a charge of -2. The overall charge of an ionic compound must be equal to 0 (neutral).

  1. Find the lowest common multiple for the charges \( 3 \times |-2| = 6 \)
  2. Divide the multiple by the positive charge to get the number of metallic atoms \( 6 \div 3 = 2 \)
  3. Divide the multiple by the negative charge to get the number of nonmetallic atoms \( 6 \div |-2| = 3 \)
  4. Confirm that the sum of the metal and nonmetal charges equals 0 \( 3 \times 2 + -2 \times 3 = 0 \)

Molar Ratios

Balanced chemical equations have molar ratios. These ratios show how many reactant molecules are required per product molecule.

The law of conservation of mass states that the mass of reactants = mass of products in any chemical reaction.

The total number of reactant moles is equal to the total number of product moles.

\( m_{reactants} = m_{products} \)

  1. Calculate the relative atomic mass of the known chemical in grams per mole as \( 44 \textrm{ g} \textrm{ mol}^{-1} \)
  2. Divide the known mass by the relative atomic mass to get the number of moles as \( 2 \textrm{ mol} \)
  3. Divide the number of moles by the number of CO2 molecules to get the moles per molecule as \( 1 \textrm{ mol} \)
  4. Multiply by the relative atomic mass of the unknown chemical to get the mass per molecule as \( 32 \textrm{ g} \)
  5. Multiply by the number of O2 molecules to get the total unknown mass as \( 32 \textrm{ g} \)

The mass ratio is equal to the molecular mass ratio times the molar ratio.
