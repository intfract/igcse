Waves

Types of Waves

There are 2 types of waves.

  • Transverse: energy transfer is perpendicular to the direction of motion
  • Longitudinal: energy transfer is in the same direction as motion

Transverse Waves

A transverse wave has alternating peaks and troughs like a sine wave. These waves have 3 main properties.

  • Can travel in a vacuum
  • Travel slower in denser mediums
  • Transfer energy perpendicular to motion

Waves in the electromagnetic spectrum are all transverse waves. These waves have the same velocity but different frequencies and wavelengths.

  1. Radio Waves (Lowest, Longest)
  2. Micro Waves
  3. Infrared Rays
  4. Visible Light
  5. Ultraviolet Rays
  6. X Rays
  7. Gamma Rays (Highest, Shortest)

Longitudinal Waves

Sound is an example of a longitudinal wave. Longitudinal waves have 3 properties.

  • Never travel in a vacuum
  • Travel faster in denser mediums
  • Transfer energy in the same direction as motion

Light

Light is a transverse wave. There are 3 main concepts concerning light.

  • Reflection
  • Refraction
  • Total Internal Reflection

Reflection

Reflection occurs when light rays bounce off an opaque object. Light rays can be absorbed, reflected, or refracted.

There are 2 names for light rays during reflection.

  • Incident Ray: the incoming ray that hits the surface
  • Reflected Ray: the ray that bounces off the surface

The normal is an imaginary line that is perpendicular to the surface of the object.
