Algebra

Expressions

Variables or unknown values, such as a number, are assigned a single letter in algebra. This is the universal standard. However, sometimes it is acceptable to use words or ? to denote a variable in other topics like geometry.

Algebraic Vocabulary

Parts of an expression have names in algebra.

  • Constant: a value that never changes like \( 1 \)
  • Variable: a value that is unknown or can change like \( x \)
  • Coefficient: a value that is multiplied by a variable like the 2 in \( 2x \)
  • Exponent: the number of repeated multiplications like the x in \( 2^x \)
  • Base: the value that is being raised to the power of an exponent like the x in \( x^y \)

Order of Operations

A basic algebraic expression can have 6 operations. Some operations have a higher priority than others. BEDMAS is a common acronym used to remember the order of operations.

  • Brackets: \( (x) \)
  • Exponents: \( x^y \)
  • Division: \( \frac{x}{y} \) or \( xy^{-1} \)
  • Multiplication: \( xy \) or \( x \times y \)
  • Addition: \( x+y \)
  • Subtraction: \( x-y \)

Equations

Algebra allows people to solve problems quantitatively. Equations can be rearranged to solve for an unknown value. An equation has 3 essential parts.

  • 1 Equals Sign
  • 2 Expressions
    • Left Expression
    • Right Expression
  • 1+ Variables

The equals sign represents balance. It means that both sides of the equation have the same value. This particular equation contains 2 variables. Equations with multiple unknowns are called simultaneous equations.

\( x^2+y^2=5 \)

The expression \( x^2+y^2 \) is called the left-hand side because it is on the left of the = sign. The equation remains true even if both sides are swapped due to the symmetry of equality. There are different types and variations of equaitons. Variations like inequalities are not symmetrical.

Variations

  • Equations: symmetric =
  • Inequalities: asymmetric < or > or ≤ or ≥
  • Inequations: symmetric ≠

Types

  • Linear: the maximum exponent for a variable is 1 like \( 2x-1=1 \)
  • Polynomial: the exponent for a variable is greater than 1 even when simplified like \( x^2+2x+1=0 \)
  • Exponential: the variable is an exponent like \( 2^{x+1}-1=255 \)
  • Circular: the equation resembles the pythagorean theorem like \( x^2+y^2=r^2 \)

Linear Equations

The most important rule in solving equations is maintaining equality. Any operation performed on one side of the equation must be reflected with an equal operation on the other side of the equation. Reverse BEDMAS can be used to isolate a variable.

Polynomials

A polynomial is usually an expression or equation where there is at least one variable raised to the power of 1 or greater. Polynomials tend to not include exponential functions. They are usually

  • Quadratic: a 2nd degree polynomial like \( ax^2+bx+c \)
  • Cubic: a 3rd degree polynomial like \( ax^3+bx^2+cx+d \)

A 4th degree polynomial is called a quartic. A biquadratic is a special kind of quartic equation that can be solved like a quadratic.

Polynomials can be expressed as a sum of a variable raised to different powers.

\( \sum p_{n}x^{n} \)

For example, \( p_0 \) would be the constant while \( p_1 \) would be the coefficient of \( x \).

Quadratic Equations

Quadratic equations can have either 0, 1, or 2 real solutions. There are 3 ways to solve a quadratic equation.

  • Factorisation
  • Completing the Square
  • The Quadratic Formula
